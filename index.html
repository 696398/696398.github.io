Unit Descriptons.

For the three units we have written blog posts for, we have given a description about what each unit is about.

Motion Dynamics.

Motion Dynamics is essentially a unit that puts the rest of the world into perspective. We can understand how forces affect motion in predictable and quantifiable ways and how forces acting on an object will determine the object's motion. Additionally, we are able to comprehend how many technologies that utilize the principles of motion dynamics have societal and environmental implications. That is just a brief description of the exciting knowledge within the motion dynamics unit. To read more, please check out the blog section, where you will be able to dive deep into understanding the unit of motion dynamics and its applications.

Energy/Momentum.

Energy and momentum are often said to be the ‘unseen portions of physics,’ but the humorous aspect is that energy and momentum are used in our daily life, whether we know it or not. Within this unit, we understood how energy and momentum are conserved in all interactions and understand how interactions involving the laws of conservation of energy and conservation of momentum can be analyzed mathematically. Furthermore, we were able to view some technological applications that involve energy and momentum, and how these applications can affect the society and the environment in positive and negative ways. That is just a brief description of the exciting knowledge within the energy and momentum unit. To read more, please check out the blog section, where you will be able to dive deep into understanding the unit of energy and momentum and its applications.

Fields.

Fields are often known as one of the most exciting physics units as special objects, and circumstances lead to fields being generated. In this unit, we learn about how gravitational, electric and magnetic forces act on matter from a distance. We were also able to distinguish many of the similar properties between gravitational, electric, and magnetic fields. One of the more important topics in this unit was how we understood that the behaviour of matter in gravitational, electric, and magnetic fields can be described mathematically. Last but not least, we saw how technological systems involving gravitational, electric, and magnetic fields can affect society and the environment. That is just a brief description of the exciting knowledge within the fields unit. To read more, please check out the blog section, where you will be able to dive deep into understanding the unit of fields and its applications.
